## The problem

Before School Sport Portal, Williamstown Primary's sport admin looked like every other primary school's:

- A spreadsheet of students copy-pasted from Compass each February.
- A second spreadsheet for swimming carnival entries.
- A third spreadsheet for athletics carnival.
- A fourth for cross-country.
- A clipboard of finish times on race day, transcribed by hand into the spreadsheet that night.
- A printed-out PDF of results pinned to the staffroom noticeboard the following day.

Each sport meant another export, another import, another round of "is this kid in the right age group?" arguments at sign-on.

District-level was worse. Williamstown District Cross-Country brings 8 schools and 192 runners to a single morning at McIvor Reserve. The convening school had to collect Excel sheets from every other school, paste them into a master spreadsheet, generate bib numbers manually, and reconcile times scribbled by lane judges with a master clock kept on someone's phone.

By the time the results were emailed out to other schools' coordinators, it was Friday evening. Parents heard times by word of mouth from their kids on Thursday afternoon and were mostly wrong.

---

## What changed

WPS uploaded its student roster once into School Sport Portal in early 2026. From that single roster:

- **Cross-country house event** — random bib allocation, lane assignments, and finishing-line marshal sheets generated in 5 minutes.
- **District XC carnival** — eight participating schools' rosters loaded into one shared timing app. Each school's PE coordinator set their entries from the master roster — no spreadsheet exchange, no re-keying.
- **Race-day** — Carnival Timing app on a phone at the finish line. Marshal taps a 4-digit bib, the time is captured, the runner appears on the public results page within seconds.
- **No-bibs fallback** — when bibs went missing from a heat, the marshal tapped a name picker filtered to runners in the active race. Identification took 2–3 seconds per finisher.
- **Public results** — sportcarnival.com.au/wd26 updates live as each finisher crosses the line. Parents at the side of the course refresh and see their kid's time before the next race finishes.

## What WPS uses now

- **School Sport Portal** — single roster, sport calendar, parent-view, end-of-season reporting.
- **Carnival Timing** — race-day app with Marshal mode, Lane Timer, Video Finish, Spectator view. House-points engine for athletics carnivals.
- **SportCarnival** — public results page at sportcarnival.com.au/wd26 (Williamstown District) and a non-public school page at /williamstownps for school-level events.

The same platform now serves WPS as a **school**, the **Williamstown District** convener, and the **Hobsons Bay Division**. Same roster. Same bib numbers. Same scoring engine. Same public page.

---

## What other schools should know

If you run district sport for a cluster of primary schools, **the convening school benefits most**. SSP eliminates the cross-school spreadsheet exchange entirely. If you don't convene, you still get to keep your own roster from year to year and never re-enter a student who's already on the platform from a previous carnival.

Most schools join because their PE coordinator is sick of the spreadsheet dance. They stay because parents stop asking what their kid's time was — they can already see it.
